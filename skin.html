Skin

The skin is the organ that covers the human body and the bodies of many other animals. main function of the skin in humans is to protect the body and is one of the first lines of defense against germs, and the skin protects the body through its physical properties, as it is almost completely resistant to wetness, and prevents the permeation of fluids that submerge the tissues of the body. The skin is the largest organ in the human body.
And the skin prevents bacteria and chemicals from entering most parts of the body, and protects the tissues that lie beneath it from the harmful rays of the sun.
In addition, the skin helps maintain the internal temperature of the body at normal levels, by making the glands in the skin secrete sweat when a person is exposed to intense heat, as the sweat evaporates, cooling the body; But when the cold is severe, the body retains heat by narrowing the blood vessels in the skin, and as a result, the blood flow to the surface of the body decreases, and thus the body loses less heat.
In the skin, there are many nerve endings that are sensitive to cold and heat, as well as the nerve endings of pain, pressure and touch.
The skin is the largest organ in the human body. If we spread the skin of a male human weighing 6-8 kg, it would cover an area of about two square metres.
The skin, in anatomy , is the protective outer covering of the body . The skin is the largest organ in the human body . The skin consists of 3 main layers:
Epidermis : the outer layer of the skin. This layer contains the melanin cells responsible for skin color Dermis : The dermis under the epidermis contains nerve endings, sweat and sebaceous glands, blood vessels and lymph.

Skin structure

The skin is characterized by the presence of three layers of tissue:
skin. dermis Subcutaneous tissue or (adipose tissue). The epidermis, which is the outer surface layer, is the thickness of a sheet, and the skin covers most parts of the body. The thickness of the dermis, which is the middle layer, is about 15-40 times the thickness of the epidermis.
As for the subcutaneous tissue, which is the inner layer, it varies greatly in thickness among individuals, but in all people it is much thicker than both the epidermis and the dermis. In addition to these tissues, the skin includes hair, nails, and certain types of glands.

skin color

Skin color varies greatly between peoples and individuals. The color of the skin depends mainly on the amount of brown pigment melanin produced by the skin, and melanin is formed by the cells that produce it in the skin. People of all nations are equal in the number of these cells, but the melanin-producing cells in people with dark skin produce more melanin than in people with light skin, and the amount of melanin produced in each person depends mainly on heredity. However, exposure to sunlight increases melanin production and leads to lighter skin pigmentation. In some cases, melanin forms and collects in small spots, forming freckles, most of which appear on the face and hands, and exposure to sunlight may lead to an increase in freckles.
With age, melanin-producing cells produce it in an irregular way, so that some areas of the skin remain light while others become dark. These dark spots are sometimes called age spots (pyramid) or liver spots. The skin also becomes thinner and drier with increasing age. Therefore, the skin begins to wrinkle and becomes covered with scales or scales. In addition, the skin of an elderly person is injured, bruised and cracked with unusual ease, and it heals and heals very slowly.

Skin disorders

Skin infections; The most common skin infection is eczema, in which the skin reddens and provokes the urge to itch, and the skin may be covered with small scales or leaking liquid from it. And hypersensitivity eczema is a common disease in children and appears in most cases on the face, back of the neck, knees or inner sides of the arms. Contact dermatitis is a type of allergy to certain substances that a person touches. Many people, for example, develop a rash after being stabbed with nettles, and after prolonged use and contact with some chemicals such as acids, dyes, and some types of soap and medicines, or after wearing some types of shoes or underwear made of synthetic fibers. Or when dealing with hard building materials such as cement.
infection A skin infection is caused by a type of bacteria, fungi, parasites, or virus. Some of these organisms invade the body by penetrating the skin and others remain on the surface of the skin. Some types of infections, such as boils and impetigo, are caused by bacteria. Boils are painful red bumps filled with pus. Impetigo, which mainly affects children, leads to the appearance of thin blisters on the skin, which crack or burst and the skin becomes scaly or covered with scales.
Fungi cause diseases such as ringworm (ringworm) and athlete's foot. Ringworm is a general name for many types of fungal infections, the common type of which is characterized by the appearance of areas of red, ring-shaped spots with thin scales. As for athlete's foot, it is a type of ringworm in which cracks form in the skin between the toes.
Parasites that infect the skin include lice and scabies mites. Lice invade the hair of the scalp and other parts of the body, while the scabies mite burrows and hides under the surface of the skin. And both are contagious and lead to itching or itching.
Viruses cause cold sores and herpes zoster, as well as other types of diseases. Cold sores are blisters that usually appear around the mouth. Herpes zoster is painful blisters that first appear on the chest and lower back.
burns; Burns are caused by heat from a fire or other source, some chemicals, electric shock, or excessive exposure to sunlight. Doctors divide burns into three degrees: first-degree burns, second-degree burns, and third-degree burns. First-degree burns lead to redness of the skin, and they only affect the skin, and they heal without leaving a scar or residual trace. Second-degree burns cause blisters and ulcers in the skin because they affect the epidermis and part of the dermis, and therefore, they leave slight scars. Third-degree burns cause severe blisters or blackening of the skin. Because it destroys all three layers of skin, some victims resort to surgery to remove dead tissue and repair the skin. The surgeon may perform a skin graft in which healthy skin replaces the burned tissue. See: skin grafting.
The sunburn may be simple or severe. Minor burns lead to redness of the skin, which disappears after a few days. Severe burns lead to blisters and ulcers in the skin, and may be accompanied by chills, dizziness and fever. Repeated exposure to sunburn for a long time may contribute to severe wrinkles and skin cancer.
Sunburn can be avoided by using reflective or sun-blocking paints that block the burning rays, or by gradual exposure to the sun, which leads to skin pigmentation; However, repeatedly dyeing the skin in this way may also contribute to the formation of wrinkles and skin cancer.
neoplasms; Abnormal cell growth, which may be benign or malignant. Benign tumors are not cancerous and do not spread in the body, while malignant tumors are cancerous and can invade the surrounding tissues and spread to distant places in the body through the circulatory system, and this process is called metastasis.
Benign skin tumors include lipomas, moles, and warts. A lipoma is a large, soft mass of fat under the surface of the skin. A mole is a group of colored cells that are flat or raised spots on the skin and are mostly black or brown. As for warts caused by viruses, they can appear anywhere on the skin, and most of them are raised from the surface of the skin and are rough, dry and do not cause pain. However, plantar warts that grow on the bottom surface of the feet sometimes press on some nerve endings in the dermis and are painful.
Malignant skin tumors include: basal cell epithelium, squamous cell carcinoma, and melanoma. The most common malignant cutaneous neoplasm is basal cell epithelium, which begins as a small, pinkish bump or lump that slowly enlarges. This type invades and destroys the healthy tissue around it, but it does not move.
Squamous cell carcinoma begins as an enlargement of the skin or a bump and swelling that erodes to form an ulcer with a crust, and this type of cancer metastasizes in some cases.
As for melanoma, it is the most dangerous type of skin cancer because it often moves from one place to another in the body with the formation of metastases. A red area, brown spots or a white ring may appear around the mole, and the tumor may be flat or raised from the surface of the skin and vary in size and color. In most cases, the patient can be cured and cured if the diagnosis and treatment is done in the early stages. Although most skin tumors are harmless, any unusual growth should be seen by a doctor immediately.
Other skin disorders. These include acne, corns, urticaria, psoriasis, and scleroderma.
Acne mostly affects adolescents, and it consists of blackheads and other pimples that appear mainly on the face, upper chest and back. A corn is a painful enlargement of the skin of the foot, most of which results from pressure or friction from uncomfortable shoes.
Hives are small white or red spots as a reaction or allergy to certain foods or medicines. Psoriasis is characterized by thick, red, raised patches of skin covered with silvery-white scales. Scleroderma is a hardening and swelling of the thickness of the skin, especially the skin of the hands and fingers, caused by an increase in the secretion of collagen, a protein found in the connective tissues, and painful sores may appear in the affected parts.

Skin functions

The skin is the body's first line of defense against microbes and external factors. The fatty layer in the skin acts as a heat insulator, and the sweat glands maintains the body temperature from rising above normal body temperature by secreting sweat . It is believed that the sebaceous glands have anti-bacterial properties. It also contains nerve receptors for sensations of heat, cold, pressure and touch. The skin plays a key role in the formation of vitamin D , as vitamin D is formed from exposure of the skin to the ultraviolet rays of the sun, The secretion of melanin in the skin protects the skin from ultraviolet rays and reduces the risk of skin cancer It is the first wall of protection from viruses in the body.

Types of human skin

ry skin : It is characterized by a tight appearance, narrow pores, a rough texture, and a tendency to wrinkle. This type of skin that has little secretion of sebaceous glands dries quickly and easily and is very sensitive to weather changes. It is usually somewhat greasy on the chin and the base of the nose, where the sebaceous glands are concentrated and less fat on the cheeks and temples. It may appear on the human body, especially the skin, as the claws of an animal or the like. This is what is known as the claw invasion of the skin. Skin aging

With age, the skin begins to thin as a result of a decrease in the rate of collagen secretion in the skin and for this reason the skin also becomes more prone to the appearance of bruises and wrinkling of the skin.
